Trail 1 — Food Finder
🌿 Adding Food to the Map
3 steps — Tap a step number in the tracker above when you finish it
▾
1

Place a Food Sprite on the Stage

In Scratch, click the cat sprite icon at the bottom right and choose a sprite. Something plant-like works well — fern, leaf, or a tree. Place it somewhere on the stage. This will be your Brachio's first food source.

💡 Try the sprite library search for "plant" or "tree" — there are some great prehistoric-looking options!
🧑 Parent note: The sprite library button looks like a cat face with a + sign. Click it, then search. Drag the sprite to where you want it on the stage.
2

Make the Brachio Check for Food

We need the Brachio to know when it's touching food. Click on the Brachiosaurus sprite, then go into its scripts. Add this logic inside your main game loop:

if <touching [Food Sprite v]?> then
  change [Hunger v] by (10)
  play sound [Chomp v]
end
💡 Replace "Food Sprite" with the actual name of your food sprite. You can see its name below it on the stage.
🧑 Parent note: "touching" is in the Sensing blocks (light blue). "change Hunger by" uses the variable you already set up. If there's no chomp sound yet, skip that line for now.
3

Test It!

Click the green flag and walk your Brachio over to the food. Does the hunger bar go up? If yes — Trail 1, Step 3 complete! If not, check that your variable name matches exactly (capitals matter in Scratch).

💡 Debug tip: temporarily put a "say Hunger" block inside the if-block so you can see the number changing on screen while you test.
Trail 2 — Water & Wading
💧 Adding Water Sources
3 steps — Unlocked after Trail 1
▾
1

Add a Water Area to the Backdrop

Click on the Stage (bottom right, the landscape icon). Click "Backdrops" at the top. Use the paint tools to draw a blue area on your backdrop — this is the lake or river your Brachio can drink from.

💡 Draw a wide, irregular blue shape — it looks more natural than a perfect rectangle. The fill tool (paint bucket) makes it fast.
🧑 Parent note: Make the water area a distinct blue color you don't use anywhere else on the backdrop — this lets us use "touching color" sensing to detect it.
2

Detect When Brachio Touches Water

Click back on the Brachiosaurus sprite. Add this to the main game loop alongside the food check:

if <touching color [#0066FF]?> then
  change [Thirst v] by (10)
end
💡 The color in the block needs to match your water color exactly. Click the color circle in the block, then use the eyedropper to pick the color right off your backdrop.
3

Test the Water System

Run the game and walk your Brachio into the water area. Does the thirst bar go up? Once it works, you've completed Trail 2!

💡 Think about: should the thirst go up faster or slower than hunger? You can change the number (10) to tune the feel of the game.
Main Challenge — Boss Round

🔴 Survival Challenge: Add a Predator

Your Brachio can eat and drink — now it needs something to avoid. Design a predator enemy (a Carnotaurus? An Allosaurus?) that moves around the map. If the Brachio touches it, the energy bar goes down. You decide how fast it moves, how it behaves, and how the player can escape. This is your design choice — there's no single right answer.
